      About

      The Reiferson Collection preserves the visual history of baseball through vintage photography, documenting pivotal moments in America's pastime. From the breaking of the color line to the artistry of the game itself, these collections tell the stories that shaped our national sport.

      Each photograph serves as a window into baseball's rich heritage, capturing not just the players and games, but the cultural and social significance of America's favorite pastime during its most transformative eras.

      Collections

      • The Color Line and Jackie Robinson — The integration of baseball and its lasting impact
      • Charles M. Conlon — Master photographer of baseball's golden age
      • The Negro Leagues — Celebrating the talent and perseverance of Black baseball
      • American Icon: The Baseball as Art and Invention — The evolution of the game's equipment and artistry
